# Privacy Policy — Space Cards

_Last updated: 24 June 2026_

This is the privacy notice for **Space Cards** (the game "Escape the Moon"). It
explains what personal data the game collects, why, where it goes, and the
rights you have over it. It is written to satisfy UK GDPR Articles 13/14.

The user-facing copy of this notice is shown in the game itself (the "Privacy"
link); this file is the canonical source.

## Who is responsible

Space Cards is a personal project run by **Kris Jenkins** (the "data
controller"). For any privacy question, or to exercise any of the rights below,
contact: **krisajenkins@gmail.com**.

## What we collect

When you sign in with Google, Google gives us a small set of profile details,
which we store:

- your **email address** (used as the key that identifies your account),
- your **display name**,
- your **profile picture** (a URL pointing at Google's copy).

As you play, we also store your **game data**: the boards (games) you start, the
cards on them, your achievements, and the timestamps of when these were created.

To understand how the game is used, we count visits and a few anonymous
gameplay milestones (such as starting or winning a game) using **Umami**, a
privacy-friendly, **cookieless** analytics tool. Umami sets no cookies, stores
nothing in your browser, and is sent no data that identifies you — never your
email, name, profile picture, or the contents of your games.

We do **not** collect: your IP address, device identifiers, location, payment
details, or any advertising profile, and we use no advertising or
behaviour-tracking scripts.

## Why we use it, and our lawful basis

We process this data on the basis of **legitimate interest** — specifically, to:

- sign you in and recognise you when you return, and
- run your game and (where a game is shared) identify the players in it.

Your email is never shown to other players: in any view that another player
could see, it is removed before it leaves the server.

## Where your data is stored, and who else sees it

Your account and game data are stored in **SpacetimeDB Maincloud**, a hosted
database service which acts as our data **processor**. Its servers are in the
**United States**, so using the game involves an international transfer of your
data outside the UK/EU.

We share your sign-in with **Google** (Google Identity Services), which is what
authenticates you. Anonymous usage counts (see "What we collect") are processed
by **Umami Cloud**, our analytics **processor**, on servers in the **EU**; it
receives no data that identifies you. We do not sell your data or share it with
anyone else.

## Browser storage

To keep you signed in, the game stores two authentication tokens in your
browser's `localStorage`: the Google sign-in token and a SpacetimeDB session
token. These are strictly necessary for the game to work and are cleared when
you sign out. The game sets no advertising or tracking cookies.

## How long we keep it

We keep your account and game data until **you delete your account**. There is
no fixed retention period; deletion is in your hands (see below).

## Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to: access the data we hold about you; have it
corrected; have it erased; object to or restrict our processing; and receive a
copy of it (portability).

- **Erasure** is built in: the **"Delete account"** button in the game
  permanently erases your profile and all your game data immediately. This
  cannot be undone.
- For **access** or a **copy** of your data, email the address above and we will
  provide it.

If you believe we have mishandled your data, you have the right to complain to
the UK's Information Commissioner's Office (**ico.org.uk**).

## Changes

If this notice changes materially, we will update the "last updated" date above.
