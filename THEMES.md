# Possible Game Themes

> **Decision (2026-06-18): "Escape the Moon" was chosen.** It's the only theme
> that exercises every engine mechanic, has a concrete motivating endgame, and
> matches the existing name/aesthetic. The full design lives in
> `docs/ESCAPE_THE_MOON.md`. The alternatives below are kept for reference and as
> a source of grafted ideas (e.g. D&D's divergent "classes" → alternate escape
> routes).


# Dungeons & Dragons

"Find the dragon, kill the dragon, profit."

One thing that makes this nice is we could think about different classes. You could progress as a fighter (discovering weapons) or a druid (amassing creatures) or a wizard (researching spells).

# Tarot

I find tarot cards compelling, both in the imagery and the meaning. Could we weave that theme in? It's less clear what the endgame is, unless it's to amass a whole deck. Might be cool if you could take a few abstract concepts and merge them to make new cards (eg. a love card, plus a time card equals an 8 of cups?)

# Business

Startup simulator, as a game. You have to get an idea and funding and customers and so on...

# Escape the Moon

"You crash land on the moon with nothing but a basic printer."

This would be something like Subnautica in space, but cards.

# Corporate madness - climb the org chart, escape the cubicle farm.

Similar to the business startup simulator, but more "work your way to the top of the enterprise".

# Cyberpunk

Create the virus, hack the system, gain the funds, upgrade your server, profit
